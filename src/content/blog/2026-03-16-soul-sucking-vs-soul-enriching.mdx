---
title: 'Soul-Sucking vs Soul-Enriching'
description: "A simple lens for evaluating whether what you're doing is depleting you or feeding you — and why intention matters more than the act itself."
tags: ['mindset', 'self-awareness']
---

There are many ways to answer this question but here is one useful lens:

- **Soul-sucking**: You're doing it for the sake of others but not yourself.
- **Soul-enriching**: You're doing it for yourself and (possibly) others.

The premise behind this is simple: if you act for others without acting for yourself, you will eventually be depleted and unhappy.

And related to this two:

- **Selfish**: You're doing it for yourself at the expense of others.

Everything can be soul-sucking, soul-enriching, or just plain selfish.

The intention behind the act matters much more than the form of the act itself.
